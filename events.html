Call the Maesters - Paper Presentation

The Royalty Has Decreed The Presence Of All The Wise Maesters Of Westeros. You Are called Upon By The Grand Maesters Of Westeros To Surrender Your Works Fasten Your Stride And March To The Citadel With Your Best Works And Forge More Links To Your Chain!

Event Description:

Full length original and unpublished papers, based on theoretical and experimental contributions, related, but not limited to, the following topics, are solicited for presentation and publication in the proceedings of the symposium.

Topics:

  • Green Technology and Energy Efficient Systems.
  • Quantum computing, superconductors, physical information.
  • Bioinformatics
  • Computational Biology
  • Sensor system
  • Mathematical algorithms, linear algebra and graph theory
  • Distributed and cloud Computing
  • Programming Practices and Coding Standards
  • Agile Technologies
  • Artificial Intelligence, Neural Networks, NLP.
  • Computer Vision, Graphics and Image Processing
  • Modeling and Simulation
  • Embedded systems and Robotics
  • Human Computer Interaction
  • Data Mining, Business Intelligence and Big Data Analytics
  • Data Communication, Computer Networks and Information Security
  • Wireless Networking and Network Monitoring Tools
  • Next Generation Internet
  • Entertainment Technologies
  • Multimedia Computing
  • Information and Collaboration Systems
  • Medical and Education Informatics
  • IT for Education, Health & Development, Environmental Sustainability, Sustainable Agricultural Development, Water Resources Management, Consumer's Right, Crisis Prevention & Recovery, Disaster Management and Remote Sensing

GUIDELINES:

  • Participation for this event is open for ALL years of engineering and other science background students. Students from any engineering/science branch may participate.
  • This can be presented as a team(maximum 4 per team) or individual
  • The maximum page size of the paper is 12. Authors are advised to follow the IEEE conference paper guidelines for formatting. IEEE Paper Guidelines.
  • The shortlisted participants are required to bring their presentation only be either in ppt or pptx format.
  • The presenters are required to submit their presentation slides to the organizing team at registration counter during the registration between 08:00-08:30 a.m.
  • The presentation can only be conducted by the presenter stated in the registration form. Presenter can find an alternative to present his/ her accepted papers, but the substitute presenter must be one of the authors stated in the registration form. Early acknowledgement is required if the presenters were to have an alternative to present his or her paper on the symposium day.
  • 12 minutes presentation time slot includes 10 minutes of paper presentation and 2 minutes for Q&A session.
  • During each paper presentation session, there will be two signals (ring sound) given by a timekeeper. The first signal indicates there are only two minutes remaining for the presentation. The second signal indicates the end of the presentation and the author would have to end their presentation.
  • The presenters should be punctual for their presentation as scheduled and register with the respective event team 30 minutes before the event starts.
  • The Best Paper Award is under the judge and the organizers discretion.
  • Presenter should be in their formal attire during the presentation and must wear ID card of the parent institution along with symposium ID card

Now My Watch Begins - Debugging

The Long Code Is Coming And The Errors Come With It! No Clan Can Stop Them; The Free Folk Can’t Stop Them And All The Southern Kings Can’t Stop Them .But At least You Should Give Them A Fight!

Event Description:

  • Total time 1.5 hours
  • 3 rounds(half an hour each max)
  • Languages: C (v. 18) and Python (v. 3.7.4)
  • First round - Python (15 minutes)
    Simple programs using Numeric and math modules and built-in functions.
  • Second round - C (half an hour)
    Functions and file handling.
  • Third round - C and Python (half an hour)
    C and Python functions that talk to each other and implement network functions (probably from a MySQL database that the user doesn't have to interact with and inter-thread communication

Guidelines:

  • The team with the most number of errors debugged within the specified time-slot will move on to the next round.
  • Only Notepad or Sublime Text 3 (with no plugins) may be used for editing.
  • Use of internet isn't allowed.
  • In case of a tie between two teams, the team that manages to get the best functionality out of their program will win.
